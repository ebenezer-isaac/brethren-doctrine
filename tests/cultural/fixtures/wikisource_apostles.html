English-language translations of
Symbolum Apostolorum (Apostles' Creed)
→
  • related portals: Christianity
  • sister projects: Wikipedia article, Commons category, Wikidata item

Sometimes titled Symbol of the Apostles: an early statement of Christian belief, a creed or "symbol." It is widely used by a number of Christian denominations for both liturgical and catechetical purposes.

A Christian tradition holds that the creed was written by the twelve Apostles, with each apostle contributing one of the twelve articles of the creed. However, the creed appears to have originated in what is now France and Spain, and was spread to the rest of Christendom by Charlemagne. The earliest known appearance of the creed is in the De singulis libris canonicis scarapsus ("Excerpt from Individual Canonical Books") of St. Pirminius, written between 710 and 714. Some scholars have argued that it may have existed in its current form as early as the second half of the 5th century.

1462133Symbolum Apostolorum (Apostles' Creed) —

English-language translations of Symbolum Apostolorum include:

  • "Apostles' Creed" in the Book of Common Prayer (1892).
  • "The Apostles' Creed", annotated, in The suppressed Gospels and Epistles of the original New Testament of Jesus the Christ (1863)
  • "The Ancient and Modern Apostles Creed Contrasted", two versions dating to 600 and 1820 respectively, in The suppressed Gospels and Epistles of the original New Testament of Jesus the Christ (1863)
  • "Credo" in The Little Book of the Most Holy Child Jesus by John Priestly Warmoll (1876)
  • "Apostles' Creed" in Creeds of Christendom by Philip Schaff (1877)
  • "The Creed" in A Manual of Prayers for the Use of the Catholic Laity (1889)
  • "The Apostles' Creed" in The Church Hymnary (1893)
  • "The Apostles' Creed" in Concordia Triglotta (1921)
  • "The Apostles' Creed" in The Rosary: Its History and How to Say It by Michael Davitt Forrest (1926)
  • "The Apostles' Creed" in "How to Say the Rosary" (1937)
  • Apostles' Creed in The Episcopal Church's Book of Common Prayer (2007)
